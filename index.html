③ Reservations & Savings Plans

How discount instruments change and where optimisation opportunities exist.

🏛️ Under EA (Today)

  • Reservation scope: Enrollment-wide (shared) or single Subscription
  • Savings Plan scope: Enrollment or Subscription
  • Benefits apply based on manual scoping selection
  • Sub-scoped RIs only benefit that one subscription
  • Monthly reservations paid in local currency

📄 Under MCA (Future)

  • Reservation scope: Billing Profile level — all subs covered
  • Savings Plan scope: Billing Profile level — broadest default
  • Sharing is the default — no manual scoping needed
  • Monthly non-USD reservations canceled during migration — repurchase after
  • Cancellations don't count toward $50K rolling 12-month cap

💡 Optimisation Opportunity

With MCA, Reservations and Savings Plans are automatically shared at the Billing Profile level — broader coverage, better utilisation, less manual scoping.

④ CAPT (Customer Affiliate Purchase Terms)

Understanding how CAPT enrollments differ from standard Billing Profiles in the MCA world.

🏗️ Where CAPT Fits in the MCA Hierarchy

🏦 Billing Account (Parent MCA)
↓
🧾 Billing Profile (Standard)
↓
📋 Invoice Sections & Subs
🔗 Child MCA Billing Account (CAPT)
↓
🧾 Own Billing Profile
↓
📋 Own Invoice Sections & Subs

🧾 Standard Billing Profile

  • Created from: A standard (non-CAPT) EA Enrollment
  • Location: Sits directly under the parent Billing Account
  • Management: Customer self-manages billing, invoices, and cost views
  • Invoice: Customer receives and pays the invoice directly
  • RIs & SPs: Shared at the Billing Profile level across all subscriptions
  • Hierarchy depth: Billing Account → Billing Profile → Invoice Sections → Subscriptions
  • Autonomy: Full control — customer assigns all billing roles

🔗 CAPT — Child MCA Billing Account

  • Created from: A CAPT (Affiliate) EA Enrollment
  • Location: Creates a separate child Billing Account under the parent
  • Management: Affiliate manages billing on behalf of the customer
  • Invoice: Affiliate handles invoicing and may apply markups or bundle services
  • RIs & SPs: Shared within the child account's own Billing Profile
  • Hierarchy depth: Parent Billing Account → Child Billing Account → Billing Profile → Invoice Sections → Subscriptions
  • Autonomy: Affiliate controls billing; customer retains Azure resource access
Aspect🧾 Standard Billing Profile🔗 CAPT (Child MCA)
SourceStandard EA EnrollmentCAPT (Affiliate) EA Enrollment
MCA Entity CreatedBilling Profile under parent Billing AccountSeparate child Billing Account with its own Billing Profile
Who Manages BillingCustomerAffiliate (CSP / Indirect Provider)
Invoice RecipientCustomer directlyAffiliate (may re-bill to customer)
RI / SP Sharing ScopeWithin Billing ProfileWithin child account's Billing Profile (isolated)
Billing RolesCustomer assigns all rolesAffiliate holds billing roles; customer has RBAC on resources
Cost VisibilityFull cost data in Billing ProfileMay depend on affiliate's sharing configuration
Azure Resource AccessUnchangedUnchanged — same RBAC, no impact

💡 Key Takeaway

CAPT enrollments become their own billing entity — a child MCA Billing Account — rather than just a Billing Profile. This preserves the affiliate billing relationship while keeping the customer's Azure resources and RBAC completely intact.

⑥ Why Customers Should Move to MCA

The Microsoft Customer Agreement replaces the Enterprise Agreement with a modern, digital-first billing experience. Here's why it matters.

📄 What is MCA?

A simplified purchase agreement — presented, accepted, and stored through a fully digital experience. It's short, doesn't expire, and automatically updates as you add products. No more annual renewals or renegotiations.

📝 Never Expires

Unlike EA which requires renewal every 1-3 years with a new enrollment number, MCA is signed once digitally and never expires. Your Billing Profile IDs stay stable forever — no more broken dashboards or scripts at renewal.

💳 Flexible Invoicing

Create multiple billing profiles for separate invoices per business unit, project, or region. Configure Marketplace purchases on the same invoice or separate. No more splitting one giant EA invoice manually.

📊 Real-Time Cost Management

Enterprise-grade cost analysis, budgets, and savings recommendations built into the Azure portal. Set spending alerts per billing profile or invoice section. Integrated Power BI with modern OAuth2 APIs.

🌐 Multi-Tenant Billing

Natively manage billing across multiple Azure AD tenants from a single MCA billing account. No need to guest users into a primary tenant — associate tenants securely while maintaining control.

🔒 Granular RBAC for Billing

Separate billing roles from Azure resource roles. Assign Owner, Contributor, Reader, Invoice Manager at billing account, profile, or section level. More precise access control than EA's flat role model.

⚡ Instant Purchasing

No upfront costs required. Pay-as-you-go billing for most services. Buy what you need when you need it. Negotiate commitments (MACC) when ready. Start using Azure immediately after signing.

🧩 Simpler Hierarchy

The Account layer is removed. Subscriptions attach directly to Invoice Sections (formerly Departments). Account Owners become Subscription Creators. Fewer layers = easier governance.

🎫 Reservation Sharing

Reserved Instances and Savings Plans auto-share at the Billing Profile level across all subscriptions. More workloads benefit from existing commitments without manual scoping per account.

🛡️ Zero-Impact Migration

This is a billing change, not a cloud migration. No resources move. No downtime. No permission changes. Same Azure portal, same RBAC, same running services. Only the billing wrapper updates.

⑧ Multitenancy in MCA — Manage Billing Across Tenants

Understand how a single MCA billing account can manage subscriptions across multiple Microsoft Entra tenants, which roles are needed, and how to transfer billing ownership between them.

🏢 What's a Tenant?

A tenant is a dedicated Microsoft Entra ID instance representing your organization. Each tenant is distinct and separate. Users, groups, and apps are managed per-tenant.

🔗 Associated Tenant

An associated tenant is linked to your primary billing tenant's billing account. You can move Microsoft 365 subscriptions to these tenants and assign billing roles to users in them.

💡 Key Principle

Billing ownership transfer only changes the invoice arrangement. Users, resources, and Azure RBAC permissions on the subscription do not change. The subscription stays in its original tenant.

📐 How Tenants & Subscriptions Relate to a Billing Account

  • ●Your MCA billing account is associated with a single primary tenant
  • ●Users in the primary tenant or in associated tenants can access billing if they have the appropriate billing role
  • ●New Azure subscriptions are created in the current user's tenant — you can select from any tenant you have access to
  • ●You can link existing subscriptions from other tenants via billing ownership transfer — the subscription and its resources stay in the original tenant
  • ●This lets you centrally manage billing through one tenant while keeping resources distributed across tenants

👥 Role Responsibilities — Who Does What

Each step in a multitenant setup requires specific roles. Here's the breakdown.

🔑 Billing Account Owner / Contributor

  • Create billing profiles under the MCA billing account
  • Assign billing roles to users in the primary tenant
  • Assign billing roles to users in associated tenants
  • Associate additional tenants to the billing account
  • Create guest users and invite them to the billing tenant
  • View and manage all billing across all profiles

🧾 Invoice Section Owner / Contributor

  • Initiate billing ownership transfer requests (Add new request)
  • Select the billing profile → invoice section where products land
  • Send transfer request email to the current subscription owner
  • Track transfer request status (Pending, In Progress, Completed)
  • Manage costs for subscriptions under their invoice section

📦 Transfer Recipient (Current Owner)

  • Receives email with transfer request link
  • Signs in to Azure portal and reviews the request
  • Selects which subscriptions, reservations, or savings plans to transfer
  • Approves or declines the transfer
  • Must be: EA Account Owner, EA Admin, MOSA Account Admin, or MCA Owner/Contributor

🔐 Assigning Roles Across Tenants

Three methods to grant billing access to users in other tenants.

1. Primary Tenant Users

Assign billing roles directly to users who are already members of the primary billing tenant. Simplest option — no guest invitations needed.

Role: Billing Account Owner assigns roles

2. Associated Tenant Users

Link another tenant as an associated billing tenant, then assign billing roles to users in that tenant. Users access billing from their own tenant context.

Role: Billing Account Owner associates tenants

3. Guest Users (B2B)

Invite external users as guests into the primary billing tenant using Microsoft Entra B2B. After accepting the invitation, they can be assigned billing roles.

Role: Billing Account Owner invites guests

⚠️ Key Considerations

Currency changes: If the transfer involves a pricing currency change (e.g. EUR → USD), Savings Plans will be cancelled and repurchased in the destination currency at equivalent rates. Monthly reservations will also be cancelled at the next payment.

Guest user security: Guest users get access to the MCA billing tenant. Restrict guest permissions via Microsoft Entra External Collaboration settings to limit exposure.

Transfer expiration: Transfer requests expire if the recipient doesn't act. The requester can monitor status under Cost Management + Billing → Transfer requests.

No B2B/B2C tenants: You cannot transfer subscriptions to a Microsoft Entra B2B or Azure B2C tenant.

💰 MACC — Microsoft Azure Consumption Commitment

A MACC is a contractual spending commitment to consume Azure services over a set period. It's tracked as an Azure resource under a subscription and billing profile. Understanding how MACC works — especially during EA→MCA transitions and CAPT scenarios — is critical for sellers.

📊 Trackable in Portal 🔄 Movable Across RGs/Subs 📋 Milestone Tracking ⚠️ URI Changes on Move

📖 What is MACC & How Does It Work?

Key concepts about Azure Consumption Commitment resources.

📦 MACC as a Resource

When you accept a MACC under an MCA, it's placed as an Azure resource inside a subscription and resource group. The resource contains metadata: commitment amount, status, start/end dates, and system ID.

📊 What You Can Track

  • Start & end dates of the commitment
  • Remaining balance vs. consumed amount
  • Eligible spending that counts toward MACC
  • Milestone details (end date, amount, status)
  • Shortfall amounts if milestones aren't met

🔍 How to Track

  • Azure Portal → search "Azure Consumption Commitments"
  • Select your MACC resource → Overview for status
  • Milestones tab for per-milestone progress
  • REST APIs for programmatic tracking
  • Settings → Properties for the resource URI

⚠️ Important: URI Changes After Move

When a MACC resource moves across resource groups or subscriptions, its resource URI changes. If any automation, scripts, or API integrations reference the MACC by URI, they must be updated after the move.

🏗️ MACC with CAPT — What Happens?

When a customer has CAPT-managed enrollments, the MACC tracking works differently.

📄 Standard MCA (No CAPT)

  • ●MACC resource lives in a subscription under the MCA billing profile
  • ●All consumption across the billing account counts toward the MACC
  • ●Track via Azure Portal → "Azure Consumption Commitments"
  • ●Milestones tab shows per-milestone progress & shortfalls
  • ●REST APIs available for programmatic consumption tracking

🏗️ With CAPT (Child MCA Billing Accounts)

  • ●CAPT creates separate child MCA billing accounts per enrollment
  • ●MACC is tracked at the parent billing account level — not per child
  • ●Consumption from all child billing accounts rolls up to the parent MACC
  • ●If enrollments have different currencies, MACC is tracked in the parent's currency
  • ●Billing Account Owner on the parent account manages the MACC commitment

☁️ Cloud Subscription — Key Guidance

Understanding the relationship between cloud subscriptions, regions, and resources.

🌐 Subscriptions Are NOT Region-Bound

A Cloud Subscription is a billing container, not a regional entity. You cannot move a subscription from one Azure region to another — because it doesn't live in a region. The resources within the subscription are deployed to specific regions.

💡 To "move regions" — you move the individual resources (VMs, databases, etc.) using Azure Resource Mover or manual migration methods. The subscription itself stays as-is.

⚠️ Why Cloud Subscriptions Matter for MACC

  • MACC resource lives inside a cloud subscription
  • Moving the MACC to a different subscription is possible but changes the URI
  • Destination subscription must be in the same billing profile
  • Subscription can hold resources across multiple regions simultaneously
  • All resource consumption in the subscription counts toward MACC if eligible

📋 MACC Quick Reference

Rename: MACC resource name is part of the URI and can't be changed — use Azure tags for custom naming.
Delete: You can only delete a MACC resource if its status is Failed or Canceled. Deletion is permanent.
Cancel: Contact your Microsoft account team for MACC cancellation.
Milestone tracking: View end dates, commitment amounts, status, automatic shortfall indicators, and shortfall amounts via the Milestones tab.

💎 Azure Savings Plans

  • Self-service transfer: Supported if pricing currency is USD
  • Non-USD currency: Savings Plans are canceled in the source and automatically repurchased in USD in the destination
  • Each repurchased plan is billed monthly, regardless of original billing frequency
  • Each repurchased plan is priced as the USD equivalent (e.g., €5/hour → $5.85/hour at €1:$1.17)
  • Each repurchased plan has a one year term, even if the original was three years
  • For three-year plans: contact Azure Support immediately after transfer to purchase new three-year plans

🎫 Reservations

  • Self-service transfer: Supported when there's no currency change or if the reservation is paid upfront
  • Currency change scenario: Monthly paid reservations are canceled for the source at the time of the next monthly payment
  • Cancellation is intentional and only affects monthly reservation purchases
